INDIA'S JOURNEY

TIMELINE

A chronological journey through the defining moments of India's greatest legends — from the freedom struggle to modern India.

MILESTONES THROUGH TIME

THE 1800s – SEEDS OF AWAKENING
1858
RABINDRANATH TAGORE

Birth of a Literary Legend

Rabindranath Tagore is born in Calcutta, destined to become the first non-European Nobel Laureate in Literature and composer of India's national anthem.

1869
MAHATMA GANDHI

Birth of the Father of the Nation

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi is born in Porbandar, Gujarat. He would go on to lead India's non-violent independence movement that would inspire the world.

1888
C. V. RAMAN

Birth of a Nobel Physicist

Sir C.V. Raman is born in Tiruchirapalli. His discovery of the Raman Effect would earn him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1930, a first for Asia in science.

THE 1900s – RISE OF FREEDOM
1907
BHAGAT SINGH

The Revolutionary is Born

Bhagat Singh is born in Banga, Punjab. He would become one of the most influential revolutionaries of the Indian independence movement, inspiring generations.

1913
RABINDRANATH TAGORE

Nobel Prize in Literature

Tagore wins the Nobel Prize in Literature for his collection Gitanjali, becoming the first non-European Nobel laureate and placing India on the world's cultural map.

1930
MAHATMA GANDHI

Salt March (Dandi March)

Gandhi leads the famous 241-mile march to Dandi to protest British salt taxes, igniting mass civil disobedience across India and drawing global attention to Indian independence.

1930
C. V. RAMAN

Nobel Prize in Physics

C.V. Raman receives the Nobel Prize for his discovery of the scattering of light (Raman Effect), making him the first Asian scientist to win a Nobel Prize in science.

1931
A.P.J. ABDUL KALAM

Birth of the Missile Man

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam is born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He would become India's greatest aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India.

1947 – INDIA'S INDEPENDENCE
1947
INDIA

Independence from British Rule

India gains independence on August 15, 1947. The culmination of decades of struggle led by Gandhi, Bose, Nehru, Patel and countless others who sacrificed everything for a free India.

1950
B. R. AMBEDKAR

Constitution of India Adopted

The Constitution of India, principally drafted by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, comes into force on January 26. India becomes the world's largest democracy with a comprehensive charter of rights.

MODERN ERA – BUILDING INDIA
1969
A.P.J. ABDUL KALAM

Kalam Joins ISRO

Abdul Kalam joins ISRO and leads the team that developed India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite into orbit in 1980.

1998
A.P.J. ABDUL KALAM

Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests

Kalam plays a pivotal role in India's second series of nuclear weapon tests, Pokhran-II. The tests make India a nuclear-armed state and cement Kalam's place in history.

2002
A.P.J. ABDUL KALAM

11th President of India

Abdul Kalam is elected as the 11th President of India, becoming the first scientist to hold the office. Beloved as the 'People's President', he continues to inspire millions of students.

2015
A.P.J. ABDUL KALAM

A Star Returns to the Stars

Dr. Kalam passes away on July 27, 2015 while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong — doing what he loved most, inspiring young minds. His legacy lives on eternally.
